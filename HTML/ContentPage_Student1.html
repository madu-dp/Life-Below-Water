Coral Reefs


Corals

Corals are essential reef-builders, providing homes and food for a quarter of the world's marine life.



Coral reefs in Sri Lanka are renowned for their breathtaking beauty and rich biodiversity Located along its coastline, these reefs feature vibrant coral formations in a kaleidoscope of colors, from striking blues to vibrant reds and greens. They are home to a diverse array of marine life including colorful fish, sea turtles, and various species of invertebrates.


To experience the beauty of coral reefs in Sri Lanka, there are several popular locations for snorkeling and diving:


  • Hikkaduwa: Known for its accessible reefs and clear waters, Hikkaduwa offers excellent opportunities for snorkeling. You can explore shallow reefs teeming with colorful fish and occasionally spot sea turtles.

  • Trincomalee: Located on the east coast, Trincomalee boasts some of the most pristine coral reefs in Sri Lanka. Pigeon Island National Park, a short boat ride from Trincomalee, is famous for its vibrant coral gardens
    and clear waters ideal for snorkeling and diving.

  • Unawatuna: Close to Galle in the south, Unawatuna features coral reefs that are easily accessible from the beach. The waters here are relatively calm, making it suitable for both beginners and experienced snorkelers.

  • Batticaloa: In the east, Batticaloa offers opportunities to explore less crowded reefs with a variety of marine life. The reefs here are known for their resilience and are less affected by human activities.

Remember to prioritize responsible tourism practices to help preserve these fragile environments for future generations to enjoy...!
